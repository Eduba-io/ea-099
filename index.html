Your January post on Lovable named the Last 20% problem. Good framing. 02

The frame, applied to your stack

The working rule we use with clients is 60/30/10. Most of the Lovable-to-production rescues your team is already doing follow this distribution. Naming it explicitly before the kickoff changes what gets scoped, what gets priced, and what the client keeps paying for after launch.

60%
Traditional code

What gets pitched as an "AI problem" that is really traditional code and a database. Faster, cheaper, auditable.

30%
Rule-based logic

Deterministic workflows with a thin LLM wrapper for surface area. Governance clears, cost stays flat.

10%
Frontier models

The real AI problems. Where the spend earns its cost because nothing else works.
